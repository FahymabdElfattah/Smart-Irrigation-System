Soil moisture sensors estimate the amount of water in the soil using properties such as electrical resistance or dielectric constant, thus avoiding the need for sampling. These sensors need calibration due to variations related to environmental factors such as soil type, temperature, and electrical conductivity. Remote sensing, using reflected microwave radiation influenced by soil moisture, is used for hydrological and agricultural purposes. Farmers can use handheld tools to monitor soil moisture. There are also sensors that measure soil water potentials, such as tension and gypsum block devices.
